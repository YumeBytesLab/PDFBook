## Which platforms does PDFBook run on?

Windows, macOS, and Linux — on both Intel/AMD (x64) and Arm (arm64) hardware.

## Is PDFBook free?

You can download and read with PDFBook for free. See the [Download](/download/) page for
the installers.

## Do my files leave my computer?

No. PDFBook reads and processes your PDFs locally. Reading requires no account, and your
library never leaves your device. The only exception is the optional
[AI assistant](/features/ai-assistant/) when you choose a cloud provider — then the text
you ask about is sent to that provider under your own account. A local Ollama model keeps
everything on your machine.

## Can it open password-protected PDFs?

Yes. Enter the password when prompted; you can have PDFBook remember it for that book.

## Does it support manga / right-to-left reading?

Yes. The reader supports right-to-left page turning, and you can set the direction per
book so manga and regular books coexist in one library.

## Can I make a PDF from images or screenshots?

Yes — see [Creating PDFs](/features/creating-pdfs/) and the
[Screen capture](/features/screen-capture/) tool.

## How do I get updates?

PDFBook checks for new versions on startup and links you to the latest download. If you
installed from the Microsoft Store, updates come through the Store automatically.

## I found a problem or have a request

Please open an issue on the
[GitHub repository](https://github.com/YumeBytesLab/PDFBook/issues).
